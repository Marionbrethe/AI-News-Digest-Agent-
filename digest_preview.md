# AI Daily News Digest – 2025-11-22

### OpenAI and Foxconn Partner to Boost U.S. AI Hardware Manufacturing  
OpenAI and Foxconn are teaming up to design and produce next-generation AI infrastructure hardware domestically in the U.S. This collaboration aims to strengthen supply chains and accelerate advanced AI data-center systems development.  
[Read more](https://openai.com/index/openai-and-foxconn-collaborate)

### OpenAI Launches Small Business AI Jam to Empower 1,000 Main Street Businesses  
OpenAI is partnering with DoorDash, SCORE, and local groups to provide hands-on AI tools and training for 1,000 small businesses. The initiative helps these businesses leverage AI to compete and grow in a rapidly evolving market.  
[Read more](https://openai.com/index/small-business-ai-jam)

### GPT-5 Accelerates Scientific Discovery Across Multiple Fields  
OpenAI showcases early research demonstrating how GPT-5 aids breakthroughs in math, physics, biology, and computer science by generating proofs and uncovering new insights. This marks a significant step in AI-assisted scientific progress.  
[Read more](https://openai.com/index/accelerating-science-gpt-5)

### OpenAI Rolls Out Group Chats in ChatGPT for Collaborative AI Use  
OpenAI has globally launched group chat functionality in ChatGPT, enabling up to 21 participants to collaborate in AI conversations. This feature aims to enhance teamwork among friends, family, and colleagues using the chatbot.  
[Read more](https://www.theverge.com/news/825544/openai-chatgpt-group-chats-launch)

### U.S. Charges Four for Smuggling Nvidia AI Chips to China  
Federal prosecutors have charged four individuals with illegally exporting Nvidia GPUs and HP supercomputers containing these chips to China, violating U.S. restrictions on high-end AI hardware sales. This case highlights ongoing geopolitical tensions in AI technology access.  
[Read more](https://www.theverge.com/news/825510/nvidia-china-smuggling-ai-chips-h200)
